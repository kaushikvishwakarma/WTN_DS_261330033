# Statistics for Data Science - Interview Questions

1. **What is mean, median and mode?**
   *Answer*: 
   - **Mean**: The arithmetic average of all values in a dataset.
   - **Median**: The middle value when the data is sorted in ascending or descending order.
   - **Mode**: The value that occurs most frequently in the dataset.

2. **Which distribution will have mean, median and mode at the same point?**
   *Answer*: A perfectly normal distribution (Gaussian distribution) is symmetrical and has its mean, median, and mode at the exact same point in the center.

3. **What is the difference between Standard Normal distribution and Normal distribution?**
   *Answer*: A normal distribution can have any mean ($\mu$) and standard deviation ($\sigma$). A Standard Normal distribution is a specific normal distribution where the mean is exactly 0 and the standard deviation is exactly 1.

4. **What are measures of dispersion?**
   *Answer*: Measures of dispersion describe the spread, variability, or scatter of data around a central value. Common measures include range, variance, standard deviation, and interquartile range (IQR).

5. **What is exploratory data analysis?**
   *Answer*: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the process of analyzing and investigating data sets to summarize their main characteristics, often using statistical graphics and data visualization methods, before formal modeling.

6. **What is inferential statistics?**
   *Answer*: Inferential statistics involves using data taken from a sample to make generalizations, predictions, or inferences about the larger population from which the sample was drawn (e.g., hypothesis testing, confidence intervals).

7. **What is skewed distribution?**
   *Answer*: A skewed distribution is an asymmetrical distribution where the data points trail off more to one side than the other, meaning the curve is distorted. It can be positively skewed (tail extends to the right) or negatively skewed (tail extends to the left).
